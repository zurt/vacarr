Research

My research focuses on the functional contribution of medial temporal lobe subfields to encoding and retrieval using high-resolution fMRI.

Bio

Born in atlanta, GA, I attended the College of William & Mary as an undergraduate, majoring in Biological Psychology. Upon graduating, I worked at the Martinos Center for Biomedical Imaging at Massachusetts General Hospital as Chief Technician of the Center's MEG system. I then obtained a PhD in Neuroscience from UCLA , working with Barbara Knowlton and Steve Engel.

At present, I am a postdoctoral fellow in Anthony Wagner's lab in the department of Psychology at Stanford University, where I also maintain a strong collaboration with Geoffrey Kerchner in the department of Neurology.
